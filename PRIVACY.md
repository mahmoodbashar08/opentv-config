# OpenTV Privacy Policy

**Last updated: July 13, 2026**

OpenTV: TV & Movie Tracker ("OpenTV", "the app") is built around a simple principle: **your data belongs to you, and we never see it.**

## What we collect

**Nothing.** OpenTV has no user accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no advertising, and no servers of its own. We do not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information.

## Where your data lives

- **On your device.** Your watch history, ratings, comments, lists and profile are stored in a local database on your phone.
- **In your own cloud (optional).** On iOS, the app writes a backup file to *your personal* iCloud Drive so your library survives deleting the app. This backup belongs to your Apple ID; we have no access to it. On Android, data may be included in your device's standard Google backup, controlled by your Google account settings.

## What leaves your device

- **Metadata lookups.** To show posters, episode lists and details, the app requests information about shows and movies from The Movie Database (TMDB) over an encrypted connection. These requests contain only the title or ID being looked up — never your identity or your library. TMDB's privacy policy: https://www.themoviedb.org/privacy-policy
- **Version check.** On launch, the app fetches a small public configuration file to learn the minimum supported app version. This request carries no personal data.

## Data you import

If you import a TV Time GDPR export, its contents are processed entirely on your device. Nothing from the file is uploaded to us or anyone else.

## Data deletion

Your data is yours to delete at any time: use "Erase all data" in the app's settings, delete the app, and remove the backup file from your iCloud Drive / Files app. Because we hold no copies, there is nothing for us to delete on our side.

## Children

OpenTV does not knowingly collect data from anyone — including children — because it collects no data at all.
